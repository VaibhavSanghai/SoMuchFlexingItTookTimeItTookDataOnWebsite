Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  3. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  2. Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  3. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  3. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Cicada

  1. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  2. Female cicadas may be attracted to the sound of motors.
  3. Their short adult life is not unusual for bugs.

Panda

  1. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  2. Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  3. An adult Panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Teddy

  1. The world's largest stitched teddy bear is proudly American!
  2. The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall!
  3. Jackie Miley has the world’s biggest teddy bear collection, totaling 8,026 bear friends in 2012
